Standard Error 
Standard Error is the Standard Deviation / root(number of samples ). It meausres how much the sample mean might vary if you took different samples. it is the Standard Deviation of the means. 
The standard error essentially scales down the standard deviation by the sample size. When you have more samples, you get a more stable estimate of the mean, so the standard error reflects that by shrinking as the sample grows.

What is a t-stat? 
Sample Mean / Standard Error which tells us how many standard errors your observed effect is away from 0. In other words it says how big the effect is relative to the randomness we sampled.

What is levene test and when do we use it? 
It tests that the variance's are equal in different groups. Use it when you want to test if variances for hypothesis tests are the same. 

How to interpret levenes test results? 
Check the p-value. If it fails, use welch's test
